PURPOSE
-------
This file is the deep-context mathematical and aesthetic guide for the Lenia
simulation engine. Feed this to an AI art generator. The organisms described

any art generated from this context should feel: biological, alien, luminous,
uncomfortably alive.


=============================================================================
SECTION 1: WHAT LENIA IS AND WHY IT MATTERS
=============================================================================

Lenia (Bert Wang-Chak Chan, 2019) is a continuous generalization of cellular
automata. In Game of Life, cells are alive or dead, time ticks in integers,
and neighborhoods are 3×3 grids. Lenia dissolves every one of those
discretizations:

  - State ∈ [0, 1]   (continuous concentration, not binary)
  - Time ∈ ℝ         (fractional timesteps, not integer ticks)  
  - Space ∈ ℝ²       (smooth convolution kernels, not neighbor counts)

The result is a system that spontaneously generates self-organizing,
self-repairing, locomoting patterns — "creatures" — from nothing but math
and initial conditions. These aren't pre-programmed. They're attractors in a
high-dimensional dynamical system. They exist because the math makes them
inevitable under the right parameters.

Multi-kernel Lenia adds a second generalization: multiple interacting
chemical channels. Each channel has its own kernel and growth function.
Channels interact via a connection weight matrix. The organisms that emerge
from multi-channel systems are fundamentally more complex — they have
internal differentiation, visible "organs," and behavioral repertoires that
single-channel Lenia cannot produce.


=============================================================================
SECTION 2: CORE MATHEMATICS
=============================================================================

--- STATE SPACE ---

C channels, each a 2D scalar field A_c : ℝ² → [0,1]
For multi-kernel Lenia, C = 3 to 5 channels minimum for interesting organisms.

A_c(x, t) = concentration of substance c at position x, time t


--- THE KERNEL ---

The kernel K defines the "neighborhood" of influence — which nearby cells
affect the growth of a central cell. In Lenia, this is always a smooth ring:

  K(r) = β · exp( -(r/R - μ_k)² / (2σ_k²) )

  r = distance from center
  R = kernel radius (spatial scale; typically 10–25 pixels)
  μ_k = ring center as fraction of R (typically 0.4–0.6)
  σ_k = ring width (typically 0.1–0.3)
  β = peak amplitude (normalized so ∫ K dA = 1)

The kernel is ALWAYS radially symmetric. It is ALWAYS smooth and continuous.
It always integrates to 1 (unit mass). These constraints are what make Lenia
stable and organism-producing.

Multi-ring kernels use a sum of Gaussians:
  K(r) = Σ_i  β_i · B3(r/R · b - (i-1)/b)
  where B3 is the 3rd-order binomial function and b = number of rings

Shorthand notation: β = [1.0, 0.5, 0.25] means three rings with decreasing
amplitude. This produces organisms with concentric shell structure.


--- THE GROWTH FUNCTION ---

G(u) = 2 · exp( -(u - μ_g)² / (2σ_g²) ) - 1

  u = neighborhood concentration (output of convolution K * A)
  μ_g = "preferred" concentration — what density makes cells grow
  σ_g = tolerance width — how narrow the growth "sweet spot" is
  Output: [-1, +1] where positive = grow, negative = die

The growth function is a BUMP centered at μ_g, shifted to output [-1, +1].
This means: cells in the "right" concentration neighborhood grow toward 1;
cells outside it shrink toward 0.

Parameter regimes:
  μ_g = 0.10–0.18  →  sparse organisms (grow in low-density halos)
  μ_g = 0.25–0.35  →  dense organisms (grow in packed cores)
  σ_g < 0.02       →  brittle, crystal-like, prone to shattering
  σ_g = 0.02–0.04  →  robust, smooth organisms (sweet spot)
  σ_g > 0.05       →  amorphous blobs, poor locomotion


--- THE UPDATE RULE ---

For each channel c, at each pixel x, each timestep:

  U_c(x) = Σ_k  w_{c,k} · (K_k * A_{src(k)})(x)

  A_c(x, t+Δt) = clip( A_c(x, t) + Δt · G_c(U_c(x)),  0, 1 )

  * = 2D convolution
  w_{c,k} = connection weight from kernel k to channel c
  src(k) = which channel kernel k reads from
  clip = hard clamp to [0,1] (this is where nonlinearity comes from)
  Δt = timestep, typically 0.1 to 0.5


--- THE CONNECTION MATRIX ---

This is the architectural heart of multi-kernel Lenia.

W is a (C × K) matrix of weights. Entry w_{c,k} controls how strongly kernel
k's convolution output drives the growth of channel c.

Example 3-channel, 3-kernel configuration:

  Kernel 0: radius R=13, reads from Ch0  (self-activation)
  Kernel 1: radius R=9,  reads from Ch1  (fast excitation)
  Kernel 2: radius R=21, reads from Ch2  (slow inhibition)

       K0     K1     K2
  Ch0: [1.0,  0.2,  -0.5]   ← K0 activates Ch0, K2 inhibits it
  Ch1: [0.5,  1.0,   0.0]   ← Ch0 drives Ch1 excitation
  Ch2: [0.0,  0.8,   1.0]   ← Ch1 drives Ch2 inhibition field

This creates a 3-body activator-inhibitor system. Ch0 is the organism "body".
Ch1 is a fast-spreading excitation ahead of the organism (locomotion signal).
Ch2 is a slow-spreading inhibition behind it (prevents self-intersection).

Activator-inhibitor dynamics are why organisms move directionally. The
excitation channel spreads forward; the inhibition suppresses the original
position. Net effect: the organism glides.

SECTION 3: ORGANISM CATALOG
=============================================================================

All organisms below are real, documented Lenia attractors. Parameter
configurations are given for reference. The key insight: organisms are not
designed. They are FOUND by searching parameter space.

ORBIUM ("sphere")
  Channel: 1  |  Kernel: single ring  |  R=13, μ_k=0.5, β=[1]
  Growth: μ_g=0.15, σ_g=0.015, Δt=0.1
  Behavior: smooth gliding sphere. The "hydrogen atom" of Lenia. Every Lenia
  researcher finds this first. Speed ≈ 0.5 px/step. Perfectly round.
  Visual: dense white core, thin dark halo, clean wake.

SCUTIUM ("shield")
  R=13, μ_g=0.18, σ_g=0.02
  Behavior: flattened, shield-shaped. Faster than Orbium (~0.8 px/step).
  Has visible asymmetric density distribution — "front" is brighter.

GYRORBIUM ("spinning sphere")  
  Multi-ring: β=[1,0.5], R=13
  Behavior: translates AND rotates. The second ring in the kernel breaks
  symmetry enough to induce spin. Leaves helical wake.

HYDROGEMINIUM ("water-born")
  σ_g=0.04, R=13, multi-channel 2C
  Behavior: unstable at startup, organizes over ~500 frames into a stable
  swimmer. Can undergo binary fission when population density is high.
  Key emergent property: reproduces.

CHROMORBIUM ("color sphere") — multi-kernel exclusive
  3-channel, connection matrix with cross-channel weights
  Behavior: channels pulse at different phases. Organism appears to breathe.
  RGB composite shows color cycling as internal oscillation propagates.
  
VOLVOX ("colony organism") — multi-kernel exclusive
  4-channel, sparse W matrix (many zeros)
  Behavior: multiple sub-organisms orbit a shared center of mass. Looks like
  a rotating colony. Emergent from channel-channel coupling, not programmed.

SECTION 4: GPU RENDERING APPROACH
=============================================================================

TEXTURE LAYOUT
  Each channel: 512×512 RGBA32F render target
  .r = current concentration A_c
  .g = previous concentration (for velocity / temporal effects)
  .b = growth rate G(U) (used for heat map overlay)
  .a = unused or available for channel-specific metadata

PING-PONG FBO PATTERN
  readBuffer[c]  → fragment shader → writeBuffer[c]
  swap after each frame
  Never read and write to the same texture in the same pass

CONVOLUTION SHADER STRATEGY
  For R ≤ 20 pixels: direct texture sampling in a loop
    - Sample K ring as N points (N = 2πR, ≈ 60–100 points for R=13)
    - Weights from K(r) evaluated at each sample point
    - Single pass, no intermediate texture needed
    
  For R > 20 pixels: two-pass separable approximation or FFT
    - True Gaussian rings aren't separable, but can be approximated with
      a sequence of 1D passes at different angles
    - Or: precompute K as a texture, use it as a lookup during convolution

CHANNEL COMPOSITE PASS
  Final render blends channels into RGBA:
    R = A_0  (primary organism body — red/orange)
    G = A_1  (excitation field — green/cyan)
    B = A_2  (inhibition field — blue/violet)
    A = max(A_0, A_1, A_2)  (alpha for bloom)
  
  Additive blending: gl.blendFunc(SRC_ALPHA, ONE)
  → channels overlap as glowing colored light, not opaque layers
  → dense multi-channel overlap = white-hot cores
  → sparse regions = individual channel colors visible

POST-PROCESSING
  1. Temporal decay: compose previous frame at 0.93× — organism trails
  2. Bloom: extract pixels > 0.7, blur 3× at 2px/4px/8px, add back
  3. Chromatic aberration: sample R, G, B at ±1.5px offset at screen edges
  4. Growth heat map overlay: abs(G(U)) > 0.3 → orange glow = active growth


=============================================================================
SECTION 5: AESTHETIC LANGUAGE
=============================================================================

WHAT LENIA ART SHOULD FEEL LIKE:
  - Biological but wrong — recognizable as "life-like" but fundamentally alien
  - Luminous void — organisms on pure black, glowing with internal light
  - Slow and inevitable — not chaotic, but patient, purposeful movement
  - Uncomfortable aliveness — the sensation that something is watching back

COLOR PALETTE (canonical):
  Background: #000000 (absolute black, no ambient)
  Ch0 (body):       hot magenta → electric white at density peaks
                    hex ramp: #2d0030 → #9b00d4 → #ff00ff → #ffffff
  Ch1 (excitation): cyan → aquamarine → white
                    hex ramp: #003030 → #00d4c8 → #00ffee → #ffffff  
  Ch2 (inhibition): deep violet → ultraviolet → transparent
                    hex ramp: #0a0015 → #5500aa → #8800ff → transparent
  Wake/trail:       same as Ch0 but at 30% opacity, smeared backward
  Growth heat:      #ff6600 → #ffcc00 at active growth boundaries

MAXIMALIST VARIANT:
  All channels saturated simultaneously. Dense organism clusters.
  Enable domain warping: feed organism position field into Simplex noise,
  warp the output texture — organisms appear to swim through turbulent space.
  Add scanline artifacts at 3% opacity.

MINIMALIST VARIANT:
  Single channel, desaturated near-white organism on deep navy void.
  No bloom. No chromatic aberration. Pure form.
  Useful for revealing organism geometry without visual noise.

SECTION 6: PARAMETERS FOR AI GENERATION
=============================================================================

When generating Lenia-inspired art with AI, these are the conceptual hooks:

SYSTEM PROMPTS that work well:
  "multi-channel lenia organism, 3 chemical layers, activator-inhibitor 
   dynamics, organism gliding on black void, channel composite RGB"

  "chromorbium variant, 4-channel lenia, internal oscillation, channels 
   pulse at different phases, biological membrane aesthetic"

  "volvox colony lenia, 4 organisms orbiting shared center, wake trails,
   additive blending, electric magenta and cyan on absolute black"

  "lenia growth boundary visualization, heat map overlay showing active
   growth regions in orange, organism core in white, inhibition field in
   deep violet"

CANVAS INTERACTIONS to support:
  - Mouse position as asymmetric perturbation (inject A_c = 0.5 at cursor)
  - Mouse click as organism seed (drop new initial condition at click)
  - Scroll to adjust Δt in real-time (slow → fast → chaotic)
  - Key press to mutate W matrix (organism personality shift mid-run)

INTERESTING FAILURE MODES (aesthetically valuable):
  - Δt too large → system explodes into white noise (render it — it's beautiful)
  - σ_g too wide → organism dissolves into homogeneous gray fog
  - W matrix all-positive (no inhibition) → exponential growth, full saturation
  - Mismatch kernel radii (R0 >> R1) → two-speed dynamics, interference patterns
